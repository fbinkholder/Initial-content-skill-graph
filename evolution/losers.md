## Pre-Documented Losers

These patterns are banned based on brand guidelines, not performance testing.

---

## [All] Antithetical Parallelism

**First documented:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Avoid (permanent)

**The pattern:**
"This isn't X. It's Y." / "Not X, but Y." structures

**Examples:**
- ❌ "This isn't foundation. It's infrastructure."
- ❌ "Not matching. Precision."
- ❌ "This isn't about shades. It's about you."

**Why it fails:**
- Reads as AI-generated
- Overused in startup/tech copy
- Creates artificial drama
- Violates brand voice guidelines

**Alternative:**
State what it IS directly. No contrast needed.
- ✓ "Chromara is beauty tech infrastructure."
- ✓ "Precision shade matching from 9 pods."

---

## [All] Generic "Imagine If" Openers

**First documented:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Avoid (permanent)

**The pattern:**
Opening with vague aspirational "imagine" scenarios

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Imagine waking up to foundation that actually matches"
- ❌ "Imagine a world where shade matching just... works"
- ❌ "Imagine never having to mix two bottles again"

**Why it fails:**
- Too vague to create specific identification
- Overused in brand copy
- Sounds AI-generated
- Doesn't validate current pain

**Alternative:**
Lead with specific pain point or stat instead.
- ✓ "If you've ever bought two foundations just to mix them together, this is for you"
- ✓ "67% of people can't find their shade. That's not a user problem."

---

## [All] Banned Transition Phrases

**First documented:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Avoid (permanent)

**The pattern:**
Using filler transitions that don't add value

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Let's dive in"
- ❌ "Here's the thing"
- ❌ "At the end of the day"
- ❌ "More on that later"
- ❌ "But what does that actually mean?"

**Why it fails:**
- Waste precious hook time
- Sound like generic content
- Don't add information

**Alternative:**
Cut them entirely. Start with the substance.

---

## [All] Corporate Sustainability Language

**First documented:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Avoid (permanent)

**The pattern:**
Vague green claims without specificity

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Committed to sustainability"
- ❌ "Better for the planet"
- ❌ "Eco-friendly beauty"
- ❌ "Sustainable choice"

**Why it fails:**
- Sounds like greenwashing
- No proof or specificity
- Sustainability-conscious audience is skeptical

**Alternative:**
Specific numbers and systemic framing.
- ✓ "120 billion units of beauty packaging end up in landfills yearly"
- ✓ "67% can't find their shade. 40% gets returned. That's waste at the source."

---

## [Consumer Content] Investor/VC Language

**First documented:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Avoid (permanent)

**The pattern:**
Using B2B or fundraising language in consumer content

**Examples:**
- ❌ "We're disrupting the beauty industry"
- ❌ "Our TAM is..."
- ❌ "We're building infrastructure for..."
- ❌ "Scalable solution"
- ❌ "First-mover advantage"

**Why it fails:**
- Alienates consumers
- Sounds like pitch deck, not brand
- Creates distance instead of connection

**Alternative:**
Consumer-benefit framing.
- ✓ "Foundation that actually matches"
- ✓ "4.5 million combinations from 9 pods"

## Rehabilitation Path

A pattern can be removed from losers if:

1. Modified version is tested
2. Modified version exceeds performance thresholds 3+ times
3. Clear documentation of what changed

When rehabilitated:
- Change status to "Rehabilitated"
- Note what modification made it work
- Consider adding modified version to [[winners]]
