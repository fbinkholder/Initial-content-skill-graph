# Pinterest

Platform-specific rules for Pinterest content.
See [[brand-voice]] for core voice. See [[platform-tone]] for tone adaptation.

---

## Platform Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Priority | Seventh. End of repurpose chain, longest shelf life |
| Algorithm | Visual search engine, not social media |
| Consumption | Intent-driven. Users come looking for solutions |
| Aesthetic | Most polished, most aspirational |
| Posting frequency | 5-10 pins/week |

---

## Platform Fundamentals

Pinterest is a search engine, not a social platform. Content compounds over time. A pin posted today can drive traffic for years.

**Key differences from social:**
- SEO matters more than timing
- Evergreen content outperforms trending content
- Users are in discovery/planning mode
- Click-through is the goal, not engagement

---

## Content Types

- **Infographics:** Educational, problem/solution framing
- **Product visuals:** NovaMirror renders, prototype imagery
- **How-to pins:** Shade matching education
- **List pins:** "X things about foundation matching"
- **Quote pins:** Sharp insights from brand voice

---

## Visual Specifications

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| Aspect ratio | 2:3 vertical (1000x1500px ideal) |
| Text overlay | Mandatory. Value visible at thumbnail |
| Brand colors | Violet #8C52FF, Near-Black #0D0D0F, Chrome Silver #E5E7EB |
| Typography | Montserrat, high contrast, readable small |
| Aesthetic | Clean, minimal, premium |

---

## Description Specifications

**Length:** 150-300 characters (can go up to 500)
**Requirements:**
- SEO keywords naturally woven in
- Problem/solution framing
- End with CTA

---

## What Works

- Clean visuals with readable text overlay
- Educational infographics
- Problem/solution framing
- Before/after visual concepts
- SEO-optimized descriptions
- Consistent brand aesthetic across all pins
- Linking every pin to waitlist

---

## What Fails

- Busy, cluttered designs
- Text too small for thumbnails
- No text overlay
- Missing or generic descriptions
- Inconsistent visual style
- Not linking pins
- Treating it like social media

---

## Tone on Pinterest

Most aspirational and polished. Visual search engine mode.

**Energy:** Educational, solution-oriented, premium

**Allowed:**
- Clean, polished language
- SEO-conscious phrasing
- Aspirational framing
- Educational depth in descriptions

**Not allowed:**
- Casual social media tone
- Hashtag-style language
- Banned phrases from [[brand-voice]]

## Repurposing for Pinterest

Pinterest is the END of the [[repurpose]] chain. Take the most polished, most educational, most evergreen version of any content and adapt for Pinterest.

**Source priorities:**
1. Instagram carousels → Infographic pins
2. YouTube thumbnails → Video content pins
3. Best performing stats → Quote/fact pins
4. Product visuals → Product pins
