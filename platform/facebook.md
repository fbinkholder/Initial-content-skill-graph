# Facebook

Platform-specific rules for Facebook content.
See [[brand-voice]] for core voice. See [[platform-tone]] for tone adaptation.

---

## Platform Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Priority | Fifth. Community warmth, older demographic |
| Algorithm | Engagement-weighted. Comments > reactions |
| Consumption | Longer attention span than other platforms |
| Aesthetic | Less polished than Instagram, more personal |
| Posting frequency | 1x/day + 2-3x/week group shares |

---

## Content Types

- **Native video:** Preferred over links
- **Text posts:** With discussion questions
- **Shared content:** Adapted from Instagram
- **Group posts:** When relevant groups exist

---

## Format Rules

- **Links go in comments, not captions**
- **Native video preferred** (not YouTube links)
- **End with a question** to drive discussion
- **Longer captions OK** (audience has more patience)

---

## What Works

- Community questions that invite sharing
- Personal stories from founder experience
- Relatable moments about shade matching
- Native video content
- Discussion prompts
- Celebrating shared experiences

---

## What Fails

- Cold brand voice
- Salesy language
- Ignoring comments
- Treating it as broadcast, not conversation
- Links in main caption
- YouTube links instead of native video

---

## Tone on Facebook

Warmest and most community-oriented version of the voice.

**Energy:** Inviting participation, building community

**Allowed:**
- Personal stories
- Warmer language
- Questions and invitations
- Longer, more conversational posts
- Relatable vulnerability

**Not allowed:**
- Corporate distance
- Hard sell
- Banned phrases from [[brand-voice]]

---

## Post Structure

```
[Opening] Personal story or relatable observation
[Middle] Context or insight
[End] Discussion question that invites participation
```

---

## Example Posts

**Discussion prompt:**
```
Question for you: when was the last time you bought a foundation and it actually matched on the first try?

Not "close enough." Actually matched.

I'm genuinely curious because in all my years on the Sephora floor, I could count those moments on one hand.

Drop your experience below 👇
```

**Story + insight:**
```
Last week someone DM'd me a photo of three foundation bottles she'd bought just to mix together.

Three bottles. $120 total. And she still hadn't gotten it right.

I stared at that photo for a while because it captured exactly why we started Chromara.

The problem was never her. The system wasn't built for precision.

We're changing that. Link in comments if you want to be first to try it.
```

## Demographic Note

Facebook skews older than TikTok/Instagram. Content can be:
- Slightly less fast-paced
- More reflective
- More community-oriented
- Less trend-dependent

Adapt Instagram content with warmer tone and discussion questions.
