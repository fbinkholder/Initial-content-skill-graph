# X (Twitter)

Platform-specific rules for X content.
See [[brand-voice]] for core voice. See [[platform-tone]] for tone adaptation.

---

## Platform Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Priority | Fourth. Press/media discovery, industry voice |
| Algorithm | Engagement + follows. Replies and quotes matter |
| Consumption | Fast scroll. Every word must earn its place |
| Aesthetic | Text-first. Visuals support, don't lead |
| Posting frequency | 3-5x/week |

---

## Content Types

### Single Tweet
- Under 280 characters
- One sharp idea
- Hot takes, observations, quotable insights

### Long-Form Tweet
- Up to 2,000 characters
- Deeper single idea
- Still punchy, no filler

### Thread
- 3-10 tweets
- Step-by-step breakdowns
- Founder story
- Educational depth

---

## Format Rules

- **Lowercase everything** (brand standard)
- **No hashtags**
- **No links in main tweet** (put in reply if needed)
- **Every word earns its place**

---

## What Works

- Sharp industry observations
- Quotable one-liners
- Numbers that hit hard
- Founder POV hot takes
- Engaging with relevant conversations
- Dry humor and sarcasm
- Contrarian angles

---

## What Fails

- Corporate tone
- Long explanations
- Hashtags
- Links in main tweet
- Trying to sound nice when sharpness is needed
- Generic takes
- Hedging language

---

## Tone on X

Sharpest and most direct. Most provocative version of the voice.

**Energy:** Industry critic with receipts

**Allowed:**
- Hot takes
- Industry criticism
- Sarcasm and dry wit
- Blunt observations
- "lol", "imo", "btw"

**Not allowed:**
- Corporate smoothness
- Excessive explanation
- Banned phrases from [[brand-voice]]
- Hedging

## Tweet Formulas

**Observation:**
```
the beauty industry invented 40 shades, watched 67% of people still fail to find a match, and then blamed undertones
```

**Stat + Implication:**
```
67% can't find their shade. 40% of foundation gets returned. $2B+ in waste yearly. and we keep launching more 40-shade ranges
```

**Question:**
```
why is mixing two bottles together considered a 'hack' instead of a symptom of a broken system
```

**Contrast:**
```
billion dollar brands. 40 shades. 67% failure rate. make it make sense
```

**Sharp critique:**
```
every 'inclusive' foundation launch that still requires you to guess your undertone is just better marketing on the same broken system
```

---

## Thread Structure

```
Tweet 1: HOOK — sharpest version, ends with "🧵" or "a thread:"
Tweets 2-8: CONTENT — one idea per tweet, each stands alone
Tweet 9-10: RECAP + CTA
```

**Rules:**
- Each tweet works independently
- Vary rhythm across tweets
- Include soft CTA in final tweet
