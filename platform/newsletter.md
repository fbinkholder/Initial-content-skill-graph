## Content Type

The newsletter is the most intimate version of Chromara's voice. Like writing a letter to a smart friend who's invested in the journey.

**What goes here:**
- Behind-the-scenes updates
- Founder vulnerability and reflection
- Exclusive previews
- Industry observations with personal take
- Deeper context than social allows
- Real numbers and real struggles

---

## Format Rules

- **Plain text or minimal design** (substance over style)
- **One CTA only** (don't ask for ten things)
- **Personal tone** (letter, not broadcast)
- **Subject lines:** Lowercase, intriguing, personal

---

## Structure

```
Subject line: Lowercase, under 50 chars, personal
Preview text: Extends the hook

Body:
- Personal opening (1-2 paragraphs)
- Main content (education, story, insight)
- Behind-the-scenes or exclusive element
- Single CTA

Sign-off: Faith
```

---

## What Works

- Founder vulnerability
- Real challenges and setbacks
- Behind-the-scenes of building Chromara
- Industry observations with personal angle
- Exclusive previews and early access
- Replies encouraged (makes it feel like conversation)

---

## What Fails

- Corporate newsletter voice
- Multiple CTAs
- Heavy design
- Treating subscribers as leads instead of community
- Generic updates without personal angle
- Salesy tone

---

## Tone in Newsletter

Most personal and intimate version of the voice.

**Energy:** Texting a close friend who gets it

**Allowed:**
- Vulnerability
- Doubt and uncertainty
- Real struggles
- Longer reflection
- Personal opinions
- Behind-the-curtain honesty

**Not allowed:**
- Corporate distance
- Multiple asks
- Banned phrases from [[brand-voice]]

## Example Newsletter

```
Subject: the photo that stopped me

Preview: Three bottles. $120. Still not right.

---

Last week someone DM'd me a photo of three foundation bottles she'd bought just to mix together.

Three bottles. $120 total. And she still hadn't gotten it right.

I stared at that photo for a while.

Not because it was surprising. I saw this every day on the Sephora floor. Women mixing two bottles at checkout, hoping they'd land somewhere close to their actual skin tone.

What stopped me was how normal it felt. How accepted.

We've collectively decided that buying multiple products and becoming amateur chemists is just... part of the foundation experience. A "hack." A workaround. Something beauty influencers teach as a tip instead of calling out as a symptom.

67% of people can't find their shade. That's not a user error. That's a system failure.

We're about six months from having a fully functional prototype. The NovaMirror creates 4.5 million shade combinations from 9 refillable pods. No guessing. No mixing. No $120 spent on products that don't work.

I'll share more as we get closer. For now, I just wanted you to know why this photo hit me the way it did.

Thanks for being on this journey with us.

Faith

P.S. If you want to be first in line when we launch, make sure you're on the waitlist: [link]
```
