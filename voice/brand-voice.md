# Brand Voice

Core voice definition for all Chromara content.
This file defines WHO we sound like. See [[platform-tone]] for how the voice adapts per platform.

---

## Core Personality

Chromara is a founder-led beauty tech infrastructure company, not a makeup brand.

We combine calm intelligence with aspirational intimacy. We speak from direct experience (Sephora retail floor origin) with authority that comes from having lived the problem we're solving.

Premium but human. Confident but never condescending.

---

## Chromara Sounds Like

- Beauty infrastructure company with consumer empathy
- Founder-led brand with taste and conviction
- Consumer-aware systems thinker
- Premium but human voice
- Smart friend who happens to understand the industry

|------|-------|

## Structural Patterns BANNED

### Antithetical Parallelism
❌ "This isn't X. It's Y."
❌ "Not X, but Y."
❌ "This isn't just foundation. It's infrastructure."

### False Contrast Stack
❌ "It's not X. It's not Y. It's Z."

### Parallel Repetition Stack
❌ Same word or structure repeated 2+ times in a row
❌ "Precise. Personal. Perfect."

### Triple Short Sentence Stack
❌ Three punchy sentences in a row for effect

### Rhetorical Section Openers
❌ "But what does that actually mean?"
❌ "So what's the solution?"

### "Imagine If" Opener
❌ "Imagine waking up to foundation that actually matches"

### Banned Transitions
❌ "Let's dive in"
❌ "Here's the thing"
❌ "At the end of the day"
❌ "More on that later"

### Hedging Qualifiers
❌ "might"
❌ "could potentially"
❌ "often"
❌ "usually"

## Hard Formatting Rules

- **No em dashes** (anywhere, ever)
- **Lowercase default** for body copy
- **Line breaks between every thought** (scannable)
- **No hashtags** except Instagram (and keep to 3-5)
- **Minimal emojis** (never more than 1-2 per post)

## Hook Types That Work

From [[hooks]], these align with brand voice:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Emotional/relatable observation | "If you've ever bought two foundations just to mix them together" |
| Unity/shared frustration | "67% of people can't find their shade. You're not alone" |
| Sharp critique | "The beauty industry loves talking about recycling and hates talking about wrong shade waste" |
| Myth-busting | "Myth: beauty waste starts when the bottle is empty. Truth: it starts when the shade is wrong" |
| Stat/authority | Verified stats only. Never fabricate. "67% of people can't find their shade [Revieve 2024]" |

## Cialdini Principles

Apply at least one per piece of content:

| Principle | How We Use It |
|-----------|---------------|
| Social Proof | Waitlist numbers, community size, shared experiences |
| Likability | Founder relatability, shared frustration, "we've been there" |
| Commitment/Consistency | Once you see the problem, you can't unsee it |
| Authority | Sephora floor experience, verified stats, patent-pending tech |
| Contrast | 40 shades vs 4.5 million combinations |

## Viewer Feelings

Every piece of content should leave the viewer feeling:

- **Calm intelligence** (not overwhelmed, not talked down to)
- **Emotional safety** (seen, understood, validated)
- **Aspirational intimacy** (want to be part of this)
- **Nostalgic optimism** (the future feels better than the past)

## Mini Quality Check

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] Hook lands in first 1-2 seconds/lines?
- [ ] Teaches something or makes them feel something?
- [ ] Sounds human, not AI-generated?
- [ ] Rhythm distinct from generic copy?
- [ ] Contains a memorable line?
- [ ] SEO keywords natural (if applicable)?
- [ ] CTA is benefit-led, not salesy?
- [ ] No hard rule violations (em dashes, antithetical parallelism, banned words)?
