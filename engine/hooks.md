# Hook Formulas

Hook patterns that work for Chromara content. Every piece of content needs a hook that lands in the first 1-2 seconds (video) or first line (text).

See [[brand-voice]] for voice constraints. See [[platform-tone]] for platform-specific adaptation.

---

## Hook Types

### 1. Stat Authority
Lead with a verified number that creates tension.

**Formula:** [Verified stat] + [implication or question]

**Examples:**
- "67% of people can't find a foundation that matches. That's not a user problem."
- "40% of foundation gets returned. The beauty industry calls that normal."
- "$2B+ in foundation waste yearly. And it's not because people use too much product."

**Works best on:** TikTok, YouTube, Instagram carousels, X

**Rules:**
- Stats must be verified. Never fabricate
- Ceiling figures: 67%, 40%, $2B+, 4.5M, 120B
- Always imply the "so what"

---

### 2. Shared Frustration
Name the specific pain so viewer feels seen immediately.

**Formula:** "If you've ever [specific behavior]..." or "You know that feeling when..."

**Examples:**
- "If you've ever bought two foundations just to mix them together, this is for you"
- "You know that moment when you swatch a foundation in-store and it looks perfect, then two hours later you look orange?"
- "If your undertone has been called 'warm' by one brand and 'cool' by another, same"

**Works best on:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, Facebook

**Rules:**
- Behavior must be specific enough to feel personal
- Avoid generic category descriptions
- The viewer should think "that's exactly me"

---

### 3. Sharp Critique
Call out industry hypocrisy or broken logic directly.

**Formula:** "The beauty industry [does X] and [ignores Y]" or "[Contradiction] and we're supposed to accept that"

**Examples:**
- "The beauty industry loves talking about recycling and hates talking about wrong shade waste"
- "Every brand launches 40 shades and calls it inclusive while 67% of people still can't find a match"
- "Billion dollar brands. 40 shades. 67% failure rate. Make it make sense"

**Works best on:** X, TikTok, YouTube

**Rules:**
- Must be factually defensible
- Critique the system, not individual consumers
- No hedge words ("often," "sometimes," "might")

---

### 4. Myth Bust
Directly contradict a commonly held belief.

**Formula:** "Myth: [common belief]. Truth: [reality]" or "[Common advice] is actually wrong"

**Examples:**
- "Myth: beauty waste starts when the bottle is empty. Truth: it starts when the shade is wrong"
- "Myth: you need to test a foundation for a week to know if it matches. Truth: if it doesn't match immediately, it never will"
- "'Find your undertone' is not helpful advice when every brand defines undertones differently"

**Works best on:** Instagram carousels, YouTube, TikTok

**Rules:**
- The myth must be something viewers actually believe
- The truth must be genuinely surprising
- Avoid being condescending

---

### 5. Observation
Point out something hiding in plain sight.

**Formula:** "Ever notice how..." or "Nobody talks about the fact that..."

**Examples:**
- "Ever notice how 'close enough' became the standard for foundation matching?"
- "Nobody talks about the fact that mixing two foundations is considered a 'hack' instead of a symptom"
- "Why is it normal to own 3+ foundations that don't quite work?"

**Works best on:** X, TikTok, Instagram

**Rules:**
- Must be genuinely insightful, not obvious
- Should create an "I never thought about it that way" moment
- Keep it conversational

---

### 6. Founder Story
Open with specific personal experience from Sephora floor days.

**Formula:** "When I worked at Sephora..." or "I spent [X years] watching..."

**Examples:**
- "I spent 6 years on the Sephora floor watching people try shade after shade and leave empty-handed"
- "The moment I knew something was broken: a customer came back three times in one week, each time with a different foundation that oxidized"
- "After seeing thousands of people fail to find their shade, I stopped believing more options was the answer"

**Works best on:** YouTube, Newsletter, TikTok (founder content), Instagram

**Rules:**
- Must be specific and credible
- Show, don't tell the expertise
- Connect personal observation to systemic insight

---

### 7. Question
Ask something that demands an answer.

**Formula:** "Why are we still [accepting broken thing]?" or "When was the last time [ideal scenario]?"

**Examples:**
- "Why are we still expected to become chemists just to make foundation work?"
- "When was the last time a foundation matched on the first try?"
- "Why does 'inclusive' still mean 'guess your undertone'?"

**Works best on:** Facebook, X, TikTok

**Rules:**
- Question must be genuinely thought-provoking
- Avoid questions with obvious answers
- Should make viewer want to hear your answer

---

### 8. Number Anchor
Lead with a specific, memorable number.

**Formula:** "[Specific number] + [what it represents]"

**Examples:**
- "4.5 million. That's how many shade combinations NovaMirror creates from 9 pods"
- "3 bottles. $120. Still no match. That was the DM that reminded me why we're building this"
- "9 pods. 4.5 million combinations. Zero guessing"

**Works best on:** Instagram, YouTube, Pinterest

**Rules:**
- Number must be verified and meaningful
- Context must be immediately clear
- Use for memorability, not manipulation

---

### 9. Before/After
Set up the contrast between current reality and what's possible.

**Formula:** "Right now [painful reality]. [What's coming/possible]..."

**Examples:**
- "Right now you're mixing two bottles hoping they'll land somewhere close. That's about to change"
- "Today: 67% can't find a match. Tomorrow: precision matching from 9 refillable pods"

**Works best on:** Instagram, YouTube, Pinterest

**Rules:**
- NO antithetical parallelism ("This isn't X. It's Y." is banned)
- Before must be specific and relatable
- After must be concrete, not vague aspiration

---

### 10. What If Reframe
Reframe a problem in a way that makes the solution obvious.

**Formula:** "What if [reframe]?" or "What if the problem was never [X]?"

**Examples:**
- "What if the problem was never your undertone? What if it was always the manufacturing constraint?"
- "What if 'close enough' stopped being acceptable?"

**Works best on:** YouTube, Newsletter

**Rules:**
- Do NOT use generic "Imagine if..." openers (banned)
- Must lead to genuine insight
- Should shift perspective, not just ask a question

---

### 11. Specific Pain Point
Name the exact, narrow pain that feels like a personal attack.

**Formula:** "If [hyper-specific scenario], this is for you"

**Examples:**
- "If your South Asian skin turns orange two hours after applying foundation, this is for you"
- "If you're mixed and literally fall between every brand's shade range, same"
- "If you have cool-undertone deep skin and can only find warm shades, this is why"

**Works best on:** TikTok, Instagram Reels

**Rules:**
- Specificity is everything. Generic = invisible
- One specific person should read it and think "that's exactly me"
- Validated pain points: South Asian oxidation, mixed-complexion gaps, cool-undertone deep skin limited options

## Hooks That NEVER Work for Chromara

### Antithetical Parallelism
❌ "This isn't foundation. It's infrastructure."
❌ "Not matching. Precision."
❌ "This isn't about shades. It's about you."

These are banned across all content. See [[brand-voice]].

### Generic "Imagine If"
❌ "Imagine waking up to foundation that actually matches"
❌ "Imagine a world where shade matching just... works"

Too vague, too common, too AI-sounding.

### "Let's Dive In" and Variants
❌ "Let's dive in"
❌ "Here's the thing"
❌ "At the end of the day"

Banned transitions. See [[brand-voice]].

### Unverified Stats
❌ "Studies show..."
❌ "$25B in waste" (unverified, do not use)
❌ "Most people" without numbers

If you can't cite it, don't use it.

## Hook Testing Protocol

When logging to [[winners]] or [[losers]], note:

1. Hook type used
2. Platform
3. First 3 seconds/first line exact text
4. Performance metric (completion rate, engagement, etc.)
5. Why it worked or failed

Over time, this builds platform-specific hook intelligence.
