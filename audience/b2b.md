# B2B Audience Segments

Audience profiles for B2B outreach, partnerships, and press.

**Critical rule:** B2B language NEVER appears in consumer content. These segments and messaging frameworks are for direct outreach, pitch decks, and trade press only.

---

## Primary: Beauty Brand Leadership

Decision-makers at beauty brands who control product innovation, retail strategy, and sustainability commitments.

### Who They Are
- VP/SVP/C-suite at beauty brands
- Innovation, product development, sustainability, or retail leads
- Companies ranging from $50M to $5B+ revenue
- Responsible for competitive positioning and operational efficiency

### Their Pain Points

| Pain Point | Business Impact |
|------------|-----------------|
| SKU complexity | 40+ shades per line = inventory headache |
| Return costs | 40% foundation return rate destroys margins |
| Sustainability commitments | ESG pressure without operational solutions |
| Competitive pressure | Cosmax/maXpace piloting similar tech |
| Consumer expectations | Demand for personalization exceeds manufacturing capability |
| Retail partner pressure | Sephora/Ulta want experience differentiation |

### What They Care About
- ROI and margin improvement
- Competitive moat
- Sustainability metrics they can report
- Consumer satisfaction scores
- Retail partner relationships
- Speed to market

### Core Value Proposition

"Chromara is beauty tech infrastructure that enables brands and retailers to offer precision shade matching at scale. 4.5 million shade combinations from 9 refillable pods, solving the shade matching problem that causes 40% of foundation returns and $2B+ in annual industry waste."

### Messaging Framework

**Business case framing:**
- Return reduction → margin improvement
- SKU simplification → operational efficiency
- Sustainability → reportable ESG metrics
- Consumer satisfaction → brand loyalty
- First-mover advantage → competitive positioning

**Do NOT use:**
- Consumer-facing emotional language
- Founder story as primary hook
- Community/movement framing

### Tone for This Segment
- Business case first
- Numbers and proof points
- Peer-level credibility
- Infrastructure positioning
- No asks for favors

## Secondary: Retail Partners

Specialty beauty retailers and department stores who want to differentiate in-store experience.

### Who They Are
- Sephora, Ulta, Nordstrom, Bloomingdale's
- Innovation/experience leads
- Store operations and merchandising
- Omnichannel strategy teams

### Their Pain Points

| Pain Point | Business Impact |
|------------|-----------------|
| Foundation returns | Processing costs, customer friction |
| In-store experience | Need differentiation from online |
| Foot traffic | Must give reason to visit physical store |
| Associate training | Shade matching is hard to teach |
| Customer satisfaction | Shade frustration reflects on retailer |

### What They Care About
- Experience differentiation
- Foot traffic drivers
- Return reduction
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Operational simplicity
- Technology that works reliably

### Core Value Proposition

"Chromara's precision shade matching system gives retailers an in-store experience that online can't replicate. Reduce foundation returns, increase customer satisfaction, and give shoppers a reason to visit the store."

### Messaging Framework

**Experience differentiation:**
- Technology that requires in-store visit
- Impossible to replicate online
- Creates shareable moment
- Reduces associate burden

**Operational benefits:**
- Fewer returns to process
- Higher customer satisfaction
- Trained technology vs. trained associates

### Tone for This Segment
- Experience-focused
- Operational proof points
- Retail-specific metrics
- Partnership energy

## Tertiary: Industry & Press

Beauty trade publications, journalists, and industry analysts.

### Who They Are
- BeautyMatter, WWD Beauty, Glossy, Cosmetics Design
- Beauty tech journalists
- Industry analysts and report writers
- Conference organizers and speakers

### What They Need
- A story, not a pitch
- Fresh angle on industry problem
- Founder narrative with credibility
- Data points they can cite
- Visuals they can use

### Target Publications

| Publication | Angle |
|-------------|-------|
| BeautyMatter | Tech innovation, sustainability, industry analysis |
| WWD Beauty | Business model, retail implications |
| Glossy | DTC/consumer brand angle |
| Cosmetics Design | Technical innovation, manufacturing |
| TechCrunch | Beauty tech startup story |
| Forbes | Founder story, market opportunity |

### Messaging Framework

**Story angles:**
- "Two Sephora employees saw the same problem every day for years"
- "The $2B waste problem nobody's talking about"
- "Why 40 shades was never actually choice"
- "Cosmax just validated the category. Here's who was first."

**What to provide:**
- Verified stats with sources
- Founder story with specific details
- Competitive landscape context
- Clear explanation of technology (outputs only)
- High-res visuals

### Tone for This Segment
- Story-first
- Give them the headline
- Credibility through specificity
- No asks, just value

## Business Model Context

For B2B conversations, Chromara's model:

| Revenue Stream | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Hardware sales | ChromaCore units (~$2,500 retail, ~$15K industrial) |
| Per-dispense royalties | Usage-based revenue from every match |
| ChromaStar pods | Consumable refill revenue |
| Licensing floors | Annual minimums with royalties stacking on top |

**Positioning:** Infrastructure provider, not competing brand. Neutral platform that enables brands without channel conflict.

- $2.17B contract manufacturer
- Piloting similar dispensing tech in Indonesia
- Frame as category validation, not threat
- **Never mention in LVMH applications** (LVMH owns Sephora, Cosmax has competing relationships)

### Prior Acquisitions
- L'Oréal acquired ModiFace (2018) — AR shade matching
- Shiseido acquired MATCHCo (2017) — custom foundation mixing

**Our differentiation:**
- Neutral infrastructure vs. brand-owned tech
- Hardware + consumables model vs. pure software
- Precision matching vs. recommendation from existing inventory

## Outreach Best Practices

### Cold Email
See [[cold-email-instructions]] skill for format.

- Subject line: number-driven or emotionally compelling, lowercase
- Goal: generate response, not close deal
- Include deck link directly (don't ask permission)
- No asks for favors

### Press Pitch
- First person voice for direct reporter pitches
- Third person for trade publication articles
- No "share our deck" language
- Give them the story, not the ask

### Investor Outreach
- Bullet points with deck link included
- Per YC/OpenVC best practices
- Business case framing
- No consumer-facing emotional language
